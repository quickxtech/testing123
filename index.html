About

Founded in October 2015, the NUS Buffet Response Team (BRT) is an informal group with a mission to reduce food wastage during catered events. In light of the abundance of catered events in NUS, there is usually an oversupply by catering companies and under-consumption by event attendees. We believe that the unintended wastage can be avoided by directing people to the unconsumed food.

Our objective is to:

★ Provide a channel for events host to link our fellow (hungry) students and staff with unfinished buffets after events

★ Reduce food wastage during catered events

Current Achievements

Since NUS BRT Facebook group's launch until January 2017, 11 buffet events were crowd-sourced and linked with the NUS BRT community and at least 30 trays of food were saved and diverted from our landfill. We were also featured as one of the Student Life-Hacks on Campus for NUS Student Life Fair 2016 and NUS Goes Lite 2017.

Our Team

We are students from NUS. Started as a two-ladies team, the idea grew and team expanded to what is now - a combination of coding experts and ideators.
We strive to make NUS BRT a platform to link unfinished food to those who need them more.

Elaine Sam
Founder

She is always on the lookout for (food) opportunities that could help save her lunch/dinner money. The name Buffet Response Team is inspired from the group she calls a bunch of friends and herself who used to ‘respond’ to clearing food waste during her Polytechnic days. She envisions NUS BRT to be an effective mobilization
group to combat buffet waste and help hungry/needy NUS students to fill their stomach.

Gracie Low
Founder

Known by her friends as Gra, she is an active volunteer in civil society groups and is a firm believer of finishing every GRAin of rice on the plate. She is widely known as a food waste campaigner and advocates for a lifestyle without disposables.
